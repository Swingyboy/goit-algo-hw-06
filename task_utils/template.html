Conclusion

Based on the results, the following conclusions can be made:

  • Algorithm Suitability: The choice between breadth-first search (BFS) and depth-first search (DFS) depends on the specific characteristics of the network topology. Neither algorithm consistently outperforms the other across all nodes.
  • AVariable Performance: BFS and DFS exhibit variable performance across different nodes. Factors such as node degree and position within the network hierarchy likely influence algorithm efficiency.
  • Trade-offs: Users must weigh the trade-offs between execution time and accuracy in degree centrality calculations when selecting an algorithm. While DFS may be faster for certain nodes, BFS might provide more accurate centrality measures in other cases.
  • Node Specificity: The performance of BFS and DFS is node-specific, suggesting that algorithm selection should consider individual node characteristics and connectivity patterns within the network.
